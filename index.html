Project: Flexibility Design with Strategic Patients and Continuum-of-Care Rewards

Inspired by real-world healthcare queuing systems, we investigated how patients weigh factors such as treatment continuity and waiting times when selecting queues using stochastic simulation and stationary analysis. This provides valuable insights for designing systems with strategic patients and Continuum-of-Care (CoC) rewards.

Motivated by the need for scalable and accessible solutions to traditional modeling approaches, we leveraged autoregressive sequence models trained on historical event tables to understand the underlying dynamics of queuing systems. This approach utilizes recent advances in AI to introduce a new data-driven paradigm in stochastic modeling.

Independent Research at Tsinghua University
Oct. 2023 - Present
Advisor: Prof. Chen Wang
Project: Modeling Sequential Decision-Making Behaviors Based on Control Data of Aircraft Pilots

We proposed a hybrid framework that integrates Hidden Markov Models to capture pilots' decision mode transitions, deep neural networks to model the effect of real-time information on the transitions, and Linear Quadratic Regulator control theory to describe pilot behavior across different modes. This framework, with its tailored setup of hidden states, more accurately reflects pilots' state transitions, accounting for factors such as attention shifts and individual differences.
Currently drafting a manuscript as the first author and in the process of patent application.
